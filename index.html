Lessons
That
Followed Me.

Every project — good or bad — left something behind. These are the shifts that changed how I work.

On Technology

I used to think Python was for data scientists. Now I use it to migrate thousands of products. The tool doesn't care who you are — it cares whether you're willing to learn.

On Pricing

The amount I collected for a 685-product migration with Python, SEO, and Google Merchant Center was below market value. The experience was the other payment. Next time the number goes up.

On Upwork

The platform doesn't favor beginners. Every connect is limited — spend it strategically. My first Upwork win came with zero reviews. It's possible.

On Middlemen

When you're the one with the skills, find a way to own the client relationship directly. Middlemen will always eat into your earnings.

On Your Presence

I rented out my LinkedIn account for money I needed. I can't fully access it now. Lesson: your online presence is not for sale. Build it, protect it, own it.

On Starting

I started on a 2GB RAM phone. The upgrade to a laptop didn't change the hunger — it just removed one obstacle. Hunger is what moved me.
